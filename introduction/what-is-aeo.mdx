## Overview

### What is AEO / GEO?

Generative Engine Optimization (GEO), also called Answer Engine Optimization (AEO), AI SEO, or LLMO, is the practice of optimizing content so that AI-powered search platforms cite and mention your brand in their generated responses.

Unlike traditional SEO - which targets rankings on search engine results pages that humans browse - GEO optimizes for citations and mentions within AI-generated answers. Success means your brand being named directly in AI responses, not just listed as a footnote source.

The major AI engines Gauge monitors include:
- **ChatGPT** (OpenAI) - 2B+ queries per month
- **Perplexity**
- **Google Gemini**
- **Google AI Overviews** - appear on at least 13% of all Google SERPs
- **Microsoft Copilot**
- **Claude** (Anthropic) - Enterprise tier

### How AI search works differently

There are a few critical differences between how AI engines and traditional search engines operate that every GEO practitioner needs to understand:

| Factor | Traditional SEO | GEO / AEO |
|---|---|---|
| Query length | 2–3 words avg | 6.8–7 words avg |
| Results scanned | Top 3 results | 50–60 results synthesized |
| Traffic model | Fat head (top 3 get most clicks) | Fat tail (depth beats head terms) |
| Personalization | Minimal | Heavy (user identity + chat history) |
| Speed to results | Months | 2–8 weeks |
| Landscape | Google-dominated | Multiplayer (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Claude) |

### The Thin Head, Fat Tail Framework

This is one of the most important strategic insights for GEO. In traditional SEO, there's a "fat head" - most traffic goes to the top 3 results. In AI search, this is inverted. There's a **thin head and a fat tail.**

You don't need to rank #1. You need to cover the long tail of queries. Because LLMs look at 50–60 results rather than just the top 3, net new content targeting specific, niche questions is the single highest-leverage move for GEO.

### Mentions vs. Citations - a critical distinction

This is a nuance that catches most teams off guard. AI models will often cite your content as a source but **strip your brand name from the generated answer text**. This means your content is shaping the answer, but your brand is invisible to the user reading it.

Gauge tracks both metrics separately:
- **Citation rate** - how often your content is pulled as a source
- **URL mention rate** - how often your brand is actually named in the generated text when your content is cited

Refreshing content with brand-linked examples, proprietary data, and first-person perspectives makes it harder for models to strip your brand from the answer.

### Why good SEO doesn't guarantee good AEO

This is one of the most common surprises for teams coming from a strong SEO background. Ranking #1 on Google does not mean AI tools will mention your brand. AI search results can differ entirely from Google rankings for the same topic - because the queries are longer, the results scanned are deeper, and the synthesis logic is different.

<Warning>
  If you're not specifically tracking AI visibility, you have no way of knowing whether AI tools are recommending, ignoring, or misrepresenting your brand - regardless of how strong your SEO is.
</Warning>
