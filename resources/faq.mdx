## Frequently Asked Questions

---

### What AI engines does Gauge track?

Gauge tracks ChatGPT, Google AI Overviews, Google AI Mode, Gemini, Perplexity, and Microsoft Copilot on the Growth plan. The Enterprise plan adds Claude (Anthropic) and xAI Grok. The Starter plan tracks ChatGPT only.

Claude tracking is Enterprise-tier because collecting data from Anthropic requires API requests (Claude has no logged-out web interface), which is significantly more expensive than Gauge's standard scraping approach.

---

### How often does Gauge update my data?

Gauge runs every prompt through every configured AI engine every day. Runs begin after midnight and complete through the morning. A status indicator in the platform shows data freshness in real time: green means today's prompts are complete, yellow means they're still processing.

---

### How is Gauge different from just manually testing AI responses?

Three reasons manual testing doesn't work at scale:

1. **Personalization** — AI responses are heavily influenced by user identity, chat history, and account context. Manually testing from your own account gives you personalized results, not ground truth. Gauge uses anonymous, logged-out sessions specifically to get unbiased data.
2. **Scale** — you can't manually test hundreds of prompts across six AI engines every day.
3. **Trend data** — manual testing gives you a snapshot. Gauge gives you daily trend data so you can see changes over time and correlate them with actions you've taken.

### Does good SEO mean good AEO?

Not automatically. Ranking #1 on Google does not guarantee your brand will appear in AI-generated answers. AI search results can differ entirely from Google rankings for the same topic because the queries are longer, AI engines scan deeper in results (50-60 vs the top 3), and the synthesis logic is different. Gauge is designed to complement your existing SEO stack, not replace it.

### How many prompts should I track?

Start with 20-50 prompts covering a mix of:

- Branded queries ("What is [Your Brand]?")

Gauge's prompt generation feature can expand keyword research into a full prompt library automatically.

### What is the difference between citation rate and mention rate?

Citation rate is how often an AI engine pulls one of your URLs as a source. Mention rate is how often your brand name is actually said in the generated answer when your content is cited.

AI models frequently use your content to inform an answer without crediting your brand by name. High citation rate + low mention rate means your content is shaping AI answers but your brand is invisible to the reader. Refreshing cited content with brand-linked examples and proprietary data closes this gap.
