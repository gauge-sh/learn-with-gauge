## Summary

<Note>
  This lesson covers Branded Prompts — the queries that name your brand directly — and how to use Gauge to audit and improve what AI engines say about you specifically.
</Note>

**Key Concepts:**
- Branded prompts include your brand name directly (e.g., "What is [Brand]?" or "[Brand] vs [Competitor]?")
- AI responses to branded queries shape perception before a prospect ever visits your site
- Gauge shows you the verbatim AI response for each branded prompt across every engine
- Inaccurate or unfavorable AI responses require a content-first remediation strategy
- Set up alerts so you're notified immediately when a branded response changes

## Overview

### What Are Branded Prompts?

Branded prompts are queries that include your brand name directly. Examples include:

- "What is [Brand]?"
- "What does [Brand] do?"
- "Is [Brand] good for [use case]?"
- "[Brand] vs [Competitor]"
- "[Brand] pricing"
- "Is [Brand] worth it?"

These queries represent what potential customers are asking AI tools about you specifically. The AI's response to these questions is often the first impression a prospect gets of your brand before they visit your website.

### Why Branded Prompts Matter

When someone asks an AI tool directly about your brand, the quality and accuracy of that response directly affects their decision to move forward or not. A poor, incomplete, or inaccurate AI response to a branded query is a conversion problem, not just a marketing problem.

Common issues Gauge surfaces through branded prompt monitoring:
- AI describing your product incorrectly or with outdated information
- Competitors being recommended in the same answer as your brand, or instead of it
- Positive capabilities going unmentioned because you lack the right citable content
- Brand narratives being shaped more by competitor comparisons and Reddit discussions than by your own content

### Setting Up Branded Prompts

Add a core set of branded prompts to your Gauge tracking as part of your initial setup. A recommended starting set includes:

- Brand definition queries ("What is [Brand]?" across each major AI engine)
- Use-case queries ("Is [Brand] good for [your primary use case]?")
- Comparison queries ("[Brand] vs [Top 2-3 Competitors]")
- Intent queries ("[Brand] pricing", "[Brand] alternatives")

### Reading and Auditing AI Responses

The branded prompt detail view shows the verbatim AI-generated answer for each query across every engine. Read these carefully. The goal is to audit:

- **Accuracy** — is the description of your product correct and current?
- **Completeness** — are your key differentiators being mentioned?
- **Competitive framing** — are competitors being positioned favorably relative to you?
- **Tone** — is the overall framing of your brand neutral, positive, or negative?

### Improving Unfavorable Responses

When AI engines are describing your brand inaccurately or incompletely, the most effective fix is content. Publish clear, authoritative content that directly addresses the gap. AI engines update their responses as they encounter more accurate, well-cited sources.

Specifically:
- Publish first-party content (blog posts, documentation, comparison pages) that directly answers the branded queries where you're underperforming
- Use proprietary data and specific examples that make your brand impossible to exclude from an accurate answer
- Earn mentions from third-party sources that AI engines treat as authoritative in your category

<Tip>
  Set up Gauge alerts on your most important branded prompts. If an AI engine starts describing your product differently or a competitor starts appearing in the answer to "What is [Brand]?", you want to know immediately.
</Tip>
