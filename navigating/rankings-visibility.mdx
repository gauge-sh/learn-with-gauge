## Summary

<Note>
  This lesson covers the Rankings and Visibility dashboard — how to read your scores, track changes over time, and benchmark against competitors.
</Note>

**Key Concepts:**
- Visibility = the % of tracked prompts where your brand appears in AI-generated answers
- Rankings = your position relative to competitors when multiple brands appear in the same answer
- Topics group related prompts for more meaningful aggregated analysis
- Competitor matrix gives you a side-by-side view across every metric

## Overview

### The Visibility Score

Your visibility score is the percentage of your tracked prompts for which your brand appears in the AI-generated answer across the engines you're monitoring. A score of 60% means your brand appears in 60 out of every 100 responses.

This is your headline AEO metric. It tells you how present your brand is in the AI search landscape that's relevant to your business. Track it over time to see whether your content and optimization efforts are moving the needle.

### Rankings

When multiple brands are mentioned in an AI-generated answer to the same prompt, Gauge tracks the order. Rank 1 means your brand is mentioned first. Your average ranking across all prompts tells you not just whether you're appearing, but how prominently.

A high visibility score with a poor average rank means your brand is being included in answers but not being recommended first. This is a different problem than low visibility and requires a different response.

### Topics

Topics are thematic groupings of related prompts. Rather than analyzing 200 individual prompts in isolation, Topics lets you aggregate visibility data across a feature set, use case, market segment, or competitor comparison category.

This is where the most meaningful AEO analysis happens. A high-level visibility score across all prompts can mask a major gap in one specific topic area. Topics surfaces those gaps clearly.

### Competitor Benchmarking

The competitor matrix shows your visibility, rankings, and citation rate side by side with every competitor you're tracking. Use this to identify:

- Topics where a competitor significantly outranks you (content gap opportunity)
- Topics where you're leading and should double down
- Prompts where a competitor is being cited but you are not (priority action)

### Filtering by Model or Date

Filter your Rankings and Visibility view by specific AI engine (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, etc.) or date range to get a granular view of where you're winning and where you're not. Performance can vary significantly between models, and understanding those differences helps you prioritize where to focus.

<Tip>
  Check your Rankings and Visibility dashboard weekly. A sudden drop in visibility often correlates with a competitor publishing new authoritative content or earning press coverage. Gauge's daily monitoring cadence means you'll catch these changes fast.
</Tip>
